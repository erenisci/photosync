PhotoSync

Portable USB-to-cloud photo & video catalog. The flash drive is your preview library; the cloud holds the originals.

Download for Windows View on GitHub

MIT licensed·Python 3.11+·single binary·82 passing tests

Why

You want your photos in the cloud, but you also want to carry a flash drive that previews them all — without filling 1 TB of storage on a 64 GB stick. PhotoSync uploads originals to your cloud and leaves a 100 KB thumbnail with the cloud URL in its place. Click any thumbnail in the on-drive HTML gallery → the full-resolution original opens from the cloud. Offline? You still see every preview.

What you get

Built like a tool, not a service

01 Two sync modes

Backup keeps originals on the drive. Catalog replaces them with thumbnail-with-URL stubs and generates an HTML gallery.

02 Selective sync

Only files inside <drive>/PhotoSync/ are touched. Other folders on the drive are left alone.

03 SHA-256 deduplication

Content-addressed, not filename-addressed. Renames don't trigger re-uploads. A local scan cache means unchanged files aren't re-hashed.

04 70+ cloud providers

Bundled rclone handles transport. Google Drive, Dropbox, OneDrive, and any S3-compatible store (Backblaze B2, Cloudflare R2, Wasabi, MinIO, …).

05 Encrypted credentials

Master-password-encrypted tokens never leave the drive in plaintext. Lose the drive, lose nothing.

06 Zero installation

Single ~55 MB executable. Runs straight from the USB. No installers, no admin rights, no left-behind registry keys.

Quick start

Three steps

  1. Download PhotoSync.exe from the Releases page and drop it on your flash drive.
  2. Double-click it. The setup wizard walks you through picking a cloud, authenticating, and setting a master password.
  3. PhotoSync opens the PhotoSync/ folder for you. Drop in your photos and press Start.

After the sync finishes, double-click PhotoSync/index.html to browse the catalog.

No data loss

PhotoSync uploads first, then replaces. If an upload fails, the original is never touched. Stub recognition is double-layered (SQLite content-hash dedup + EXIF marker check), so even a wiped database can't trick the app into re-uploading or destroying a stub.
